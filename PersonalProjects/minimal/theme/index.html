WHAT I'VE DONE WITH MY LIFE


Although, I lack web development field experience I believe that I have had some defining moments in my life. Due to family circumstances I left home when I was sixteen. Despite that, I went on to graduate high school a year early and as Suma Cum Laude. In result I was accepted to my dream college, Brigham Young University. I took random side jobs through out college to support myself and while the tasks were usually menial my hard-work ethic made me a strong asset to each employer. I bought a scooter, which is my favorite thing ever. After a few years I got married which has completely re-defined me for the better. I spent 2013 in Rio de Janeiro, Brasil teaching teens English with the non-profit organization I co-founded (Favela Hope). Going to a new country where I didn't speak the language and only with the money I had saved was terrifingly beautiful and life-changing. Then I dropped out of BYU to persue web-development, which was probably the scariest thing I've done. Even though I loved BYU, I realized that I wasn't happy in the university structure and that it wasn't taking me and my life where I wanted to go. I don't want to waste time doing something for societal status which is ultimately why I stayed so long. I'm super excited to be doing web-development. I have found my passion and truly believe that I can make a difference in development. Everyday I feel the satisfaction of my designs taking shape and my ideas becoming reality. I love being able to solve problems everyday.
